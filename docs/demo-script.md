# GCP Quantum Foundry Demo Script

## Best demo flow

1. Open [Home](/Users/nikhiljethava/Documents/Codex/quantum-computing/apps/frontend/src/app/page.tsx) at `http://localhost:3000/`.
   Frame the product as a visual quantum launchpad for PMs, architects, and technical buyers.

2. Move to [Explore](/Users/nikhiljethava/Documents/Codex/quantum-computing/apps/frontend/src/app/explore/page.tsx).
   Filter to batteries, materials, logistics, or finance and open a use-case detail drawer.

3. Launch [Assess](/Users/nikhiljethava/Documents/Codex/quantum-computing/apps/frontend/src/app/assess/page.tsx) from the selected use case.
   Show that QALS-lite is a heuristic readiness aid with transparent assumptions, not a benchmark claim.

4. Open [Build](/Users/nikhiljethava/Documents/Codex/quantum-computing/apps/frontend/src/app/build/page.tsx).
   Use a starter lane such as Bell state, Grover, routing, or chemistry placeholder. Generate the circuit, show the explanation, and highlight the Cirq code export.

5. Click `Run in worker`.
   Point out that the main app supports async job-backed execution and rehydrates the saved workspace when the job completes.

6. Save the workspace to a project.
   Then open [Sessions](/Users/nikhiljethava/Documents/Codex/quantum-computing/apps/frontend/src/app/sessions/page.tsx) and [Projects](/Users/nikhiljethava/Documents/Codex/quantum-computing/apps/frontend/src/app/projects/page.tsx) to show persistence.

7. Open [Map](/Users/nikhiljethava/Documents/Codex/quantum-computing/apps/frontend/src/app/map/page.tsx).
   Explain the hybrid split: classical prep, quantum simulation, post-processing, and export path.

8. Export artifacts.
   Use Cirq code, assessment JSON, architecture JSON, and session summary markdown to show that the prototype produces handoff material instead of only UI state.

9. Open [Jobs](/Users/nikhiljethava/Documents/Codex/quantum-computing/apps/frontend/src/app/jobs/page.tsx).
   Show job status, completed exports, and artifact links as the operational view of the workspace.

## Suggested narrative

- Start with intuition, not intake forms.
- Move from public signals to one credible prototype lane.
- Keep all readiness language simulation-first and transparent.
- Treat architecture mapping as a hybrid workflow design tool, not a magic hardware claim.

## Best starter lanes for live demos

- `Bell state`
  Fastest visual explanation of superposition and entanglement.
- `Grover`
  Best for explaining interference and search intuition.
- `Routing optimization`
  Best for enterprise buyers who want a hybrid-cloud story.
- `Chemistry placeholder`
  Best for showing product honesty without pretending v1 solves molecular design.
